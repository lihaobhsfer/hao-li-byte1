This is the Fusion Byte of the Interactive Data Science class. On this page I will explain what question I want to answer and use the visualization tools provided by Google Fusion Table.

Question

Do universities in larger cities tend to have lower percentage of Caucasian people?

Data Source

College Scorecard Data: https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/data/

Visualization: Data Map

Color based on percentage of Caucasian people.

Yellow: 0-30%

Red: 30-50%

Purple: 50-100%

Analysis

According to the featured map:

  1. Bigger Cities have more colleges and universities. Dots are concentrated at several areas. The heat map can also tell this trend. (I put a picture below, since heat map is harder to publish. Note: this map is generated from the first 1000 data points, thus not complete, but you can still see some heated areas)
  2. Colleges and universities in big cities like Los Angeles, San Fransisco, New York, etc, tend to have lower percentage of Caucasian people. The dots are mostly yellow and red, indicating there are less than 50% Caucasian people in those universities.

Conclusion

Based on the maps, we can draw a rough conclusion that universities and colleges in larger cities tend to have lower percentage of Caucasian people.
